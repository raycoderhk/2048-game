📝 Quiz

Choose the correct answer for each question.

1. What does "excavate" mean?

💡 Explanation:
"Excavate" means to dig carefully, especially to uncover archaeological remains.

📚 Detailed Explanation

📖 Word Origin:
From Latin "excavare" (ex- "out" + cavare "to hollow"). First used in English in the 17th century.

🔬 Technical Usage:
In archaeology, "excavate" refers to the systematic removal of soil and layers to uncover historical artifacts. The process involves careful documentation, grid systems, and preservation techniques.

📝 Related Words:
• Excavation (n.) 挖掘，發掘
• Excavator (n.) 挖掘機，發掘者
• Dig (v.) 挖掘（較口語）

💬 Example from Article:
"He returned from a summer spent excavating a Viking site in southern Greenland."
他從格陵蘭南部一個夏季的維京遺址發掘工作歸來。

2. A "midden" is:

💡 Explanation:
A "midden" is an ancient trash heap where archaeologists often find artifacts.

📚 Detailed Explanation

📖 Word Origin:
From Old Norse "mykjadyngja" (mykja "dung" + dyngja "heap"). Entered English via Scots dialect.

🔬 Technical Usage:
In archaeology, middens are crucial for understanding past human diets, trade, and daily life. They often contain shells, bones, pottery shards, and organic materials that can be carbon-dated.

📝 Related Words:
• Trash heap 垃圾堆
• Refuse pile 廢棄物堆
• Dump 垃圾場

💬 Example from Article:
"The horse was found in a midden, or trash heap, raising questions about why it was discarded."
這匹馬是在一個垃圾堆中發現的，引發了關於它為何被丟棄的疑問。

3. What is a "fjord"?

💡 Explanation:
A "fjord" is a long, narrow sea inlet with steep cliffs, formed by glacial erosion.

📚 Detailed Explanation

📖 Word Origin:
From Old Norse "fjörðr", related to "far" (journey). Originally meant "lake" or "body of water".

🔬 Technical Usage:
Fjords are formed by glacial erosion during ice ages. Norway has over 1,700 fjords. Greenland's fjords can be over 100km long and 1km deep.

📝 Related Words:
• Inlet 海灣
• Sound 海峽
• Bay 灣

💬 Example from Article:
"Settlers clung to narrow meadows between the sea and the great ice sheet in the serpentine fjords of southwestern Greenland."
定居者依附於格陵蘭西南部蜿蜒峽灣中介於海洋同巨大冰蓋之間的狹窄草地。
