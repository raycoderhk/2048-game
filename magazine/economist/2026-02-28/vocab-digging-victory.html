🌍 Article Context | 文章背景

This cover story examines America's pursuit of critical mineral dominance as a geopolitical strategy, arguing that control over minerals essential for clean energy and technology has become a new form of economic warfare.

中文：這篇封面故事檢視了美國爭奪關鍵礦物主導權作為地緣政治戰略，認為控制清潔能源和技術所需的礦物已成為一種新的經濟戰形式。

你的句子
dominance n /ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəns/ Power and influence over others
n. 主導地位，支配
"America's pursuit of critical mineral dominance"
譯：美國爭奪關鍵礦物主導地位
geopolitical adj /ˌdʒiː.əʊˈpɒl.ɪ.tɪ.kəl/ Relating to politics and international relations
adj. 地緣政治的
"A geopolitical strategy for resource control"
譯：資源控制的地緣政治戰略
mineral n /ˈmɪn.rəl/ A solid inorganic substance occurring naturally
n. 礦物，礦產
"Critical minerals for clean energy technology"
譯：清潔能源技術的關鍵礦物
strategic adj /strəˈtiː.dʒɪk/ Relating to long-term planning
adj. 戰略的，策略的
"Strategic reserves of rare earth elements"
譯：稀土元素的戰略儲備
supply n /səˈplaɪ/ An amount of something available
n. 供應，供給
"Global supply chains for battery materials"
譯：電池材料的全球供應鏈
dependency n /dɪˈpen.dən.si/ The state of relying on someone or something
n. 依賴，依存
"Reducing dependency on foreign suppliers"
譯：減少對外國供應商的依賴
leverage n/v /ˈliː.vər.ɪdʒ/ Power to influence; use something to maximum advantage
n. 影響力，槓桿作用
v. 利用
"Using mineral control as economic leverage"
譯：利用礦物控制作為經濟槓桿
transition n /trænˈzɪʃ.ən/ The process of changing from one state to another
n. 轉型，過渡
"The transition to clean energy requires minerals"
譯：清潔能源轉型需要礦物
monopoly n /məˈnɒp.əl.i/ Exclusive control of a market or resource
n. 壟斷，獨占
"Breaking China's monopoly on rare earths"
譯：打破中國對稀土的壟斷
weaponize v /ˈwep.ən.aɪz/ Use something as a weapon or tool of coercion
v. 武器化，作為武器
"Trade dependencies can be weaponized"
譯：貿易依賴可以被武器化
